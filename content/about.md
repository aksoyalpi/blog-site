## About Me

I'm Alperen Aksoy — a software engineer and freelancer based in Germany.

By day, I work full-time at [Forschungszentrum Jülich](https://www.fz-juelich.de/) on a joint project with [CERN](https://home.cern/), building AI systems for triggering systems in particle physics. In parallel, I freelance as a full-stack software engineer, building backends, frontends, mobile and web apps, and APIs for clients across various industries.

## What I Do

- **AI for Particle Physics** — Working at Forschungszentrum Jülich in collaboration with CERN, developing AI-driven triggering systems for particle physics experiments.
- **Freelance Development** — Full-stack engineering: backend, frontend, mobile apps, web apps, and APIs. If you have a project in mind, [get in touch](/contact/).
- **Writing** — I blog about technology, AI, philosophy, literature, and things that make me think. Check out my [blog](/blog/).
- **Open Source** — I contribute to and maintain open-source projects. See my [projects](/projects/) or find me on [GitHub](https://github.com/alperenaksoy).

## Background

I studied Applied Mathematics and Computer Science (B.Sc.) at [FH Aachen](https://www.fh-aachen.de/) as part of a dual study programme, combining academic studies with an apprenticeship at Forschungszentrum Jülich. This gave me a strong foundation in both the theoretical and practical sides of software engineering — and a deep appreciation for where math meets code.

## Beyond Code

When I'm not writing code, you'll find me working out, meeting friends, spending time in nature, reading books, or tinkering on a hobby project.
