There's a lot of noise about AI replacing developers. I think that framing misses what's actually happening.

The AI tools I use daily — code assistants, chat interfaces, search — are best understood as *thinking partners*. They're like having a colleague who has read a lot of documentation, can brainstorm quickly, and never gets tired of rubber-ducking.

But they don't have taste. They don't know what problem is worth solving. They don't understand the user sitting on the other side of the screen.

That's still my job.

What changes is the *leverage*. I can explore ideas faster, prototype in minutes instead of hours, and spend more of my time on the parts that actually matter: understanding the problem, making design decisions, and writing code that's maintainable by humans.

The developers who thrive won't be the ones who resist AI or the ones who blindly accept its output. They'll be the ones who learn to think *with* it.
