## Why Build in Public?

As a freelance developer, it's tempting to keep your head down and just ship code for clients. But I've found that sharing my work — writing about what I learn, open-sourcing tools I build, and being transparent about my process — has been one of the best investments I've made.

## The Benefits

### Finding Clients

The best clients I've worked with found me through my writing or open-source work. When someone can see how you think and what you've built, the trust barrier is much lower.

### Learning Faster

Writing about a topic forces you to understand it deeply. If I can't explain something clearly, I probably don't understand it well enough.

### Giving Back

The open-source community and countless blog posts by other developers helped me get where I am. Writing is my way of paying it forward.

## Getting Started

You don't need a polished blog or a viral tweet. Start small:

- Write about something you learned today
- Share a tool you built, even if it's simple
- Document a problem you solved

The point isn't perfection — it's consistency.
