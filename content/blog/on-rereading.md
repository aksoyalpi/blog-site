I recently reread *Siddhartha* by Hermann Hesse. I first read it at 20, and it felt like a story about rebellion and finding your own path. Reading it again at <!-- TODO: your age -->, it felt like a meditation on patience and the limits of knowledge.

The words hadn't changed. I had.

This is what I love about great literature. It doesn't just deliver a message — it holds up a mirror. The meaning you find says as much about where you are in life as it does about the text itself.

## The Programmer's Connection

There's a parallel in software. When you revisit code you wrote years ago, you see it differently. Not just because your skills improved, but because your sense of what matters has shifted. You notice different trade-offs. You ask different questions.

The same applies to ideas. A concept from philosophy or literature that seemed abstract five years ago might suddenly click because you've lived through something that makes it concrete.
